Metamorphosis

Verily, I once was a mere reflection
Of all that I cherished, pure and true
My spirit danced to the joyous beat
Of a life lived in innocence, bright and dew

But gradually, as the sands of time shifted
I found myself slowly changing hue
What once was light, now seemed so dark
A sinister shadow, I grew into

My once gentle heart now filled with disdain
For all that I once held so dear
A disdain born of disillusionment
And a bitterness that festered year by year

And as I looked into the mirror each day
I beheld a visage I could not recognize
The person I once was, now lost to time
Replaced by this monster, a mere disguise

The metamorphosis complete, I was no more
The person I once was, forever gone
In its stead, a fiend of malice and sorrow
Whose heart was filled with malevolent song

And as I stand here, a shell of what once was
I wonder at the path that led me here
To a place where I loathe the very being
That I have become, a soul so full of fear

And so I ponder, if redemption is possible
Or if I am doomed to an eternal night
Where the only solace is the comfort
Of embracing the thing I once thought I'd fight.

She was a storm wrapped in skin

She was a storm wrapped in skin,
with lightning in her veins, and thunder in her heart.
